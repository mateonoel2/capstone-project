\begin{abstract}
This project develops a solution to automatically extract, validate, and structure information contained in financial and administrative documents in digital formats, such as PDFs and images, targeting \textit{fintech} environments where manual review is prone to human error, time-consuming, and limits the service's operational capacity. The development comprises dataset preparation, document processing and validation, and the comparative evaluation of eight extraction strategies organized in three phases: deterministic methods based on regular expressions, hybrid approaches combining OCR with language models, and direct submission of the PDF to the model. The evaluation was conducted on 176 real bank statements from 11 Mexican financial institutions, and determined that direct PDF submission to the language model yields the best results, achieving an average accuracy of 93.4\% across the three evaluated fields (92.8\% for Account Holder, 90.9\% for CLABE, and 96.6\% for Bank), with a median processing time of 1.9 seconds and a cost of \$0.011 USD per document. Based on these findings, a minimum viable product (\textit{MVP}) was built using configurable extractors defined through \textit{schemas}, \textit{prompts}, and models, enabling the system to adapt to different document types without modifying the source code. The variability in accuracy across banking institutions informed the decision to delegate specialization to the user through AI-powered assistance tools. Additionally, the solution incorporates a module that stores results, manual corrections, and performance metrics, making it possible to consolidate a reliable baseline and sustain the system's continuous improvement. In this way, the \textit{MVP} not only constitutes a functional implementation for a specific use case, but also a reusable platform for other document automation scenarios in \textit{fintech} environments.

\noindent \textbf{Keywords:}\\
\noindent Document Extraction; Language Models; Bank Statements; Structured Output; \textit{Fintech}
\end{abstract}
